Source Transcription

Revolutionary War Records Section. 3-525 Department of the Interior Bureau of Pensions. S.7026 In reply to your request of received for a statement of the military history of John Hough a soldier of the Revolutionary War, you will find below the desired information as contained in his (or his widow's) application for pension Dates of Enlistment or Appointment. Jany 6, 1781 Length of Service. 3 1/2 months Rank. Pvt. Officers under whom service was rendered. Captain Emory Colonel Peyton State. Va. Dates of Enlistment or Appointment. July 1, 1781 Length of Service. 3 months 25 days Rank. Pvt. Officers under whom service was rendered. Captain Smith Colonel Humphrey State. Battles engaged in, Siege of Yorktown Residence of soldier at 1st enlistment, on Goose Creek, Loudoun Co. Va. and Leesburg Va at 2nd enlistment Date of application for pension, June 18, 1834. His cl. was al. Residence at date of application, Northern Liberties, Phila. Co. Penn. Age at date of application, b. Mch. 12, 1755 in Warminster Twp. Bucks Co. Penn. Remarks: Moved to Loudoun Co. Va. in 1778 where he resided until 1782. He refers to his sister-in-law Miss Humphreys as possessing the family bible. There are no other family data. Respectfully, Commissioner.
