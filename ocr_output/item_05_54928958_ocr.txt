Capt. Emory's company, and this declarant was discharged about the middle of April 1781. and arrived at home about ten days afterward. From the time declarant marched to join the company at Goose Creek to his arrival at home after his discharge (during all which time he was in the service of the United States) he was in the service at least three months and an half, Gen. Barney Steuben had the General command during this time. As declarant was almost a stranger in Virginia when he was drafted and removed from the state in 1777, he does not remember the names of all the places. The company marched through and by during this campaign. After Lord Cornwallis returned from North Carolina Declarant was drafted again to turn out about the first of July 1781. to serve a tour of three months in the militia he then resided in Leesburg (to which place he had removed from Goose Creek after his first tour) when this last draft was made, and went out again as a private in Capt. Smiths company of Leesburg Militia attached to Col. Humphreys Regiment between the first and sixth of July 1781. This company was composed of classes and met at Leesburg where they remained a few days.
