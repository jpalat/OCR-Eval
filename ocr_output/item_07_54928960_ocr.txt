Knows of no person living who can testify to his services in the Revolutionary war from their personal knowledge. He hereby relinquishes every claim whatever to a pension or annuity except the present and declares that his name is not on the pension Roll of the agency of any State.

1. Declarant was born in Wammster township Bucks county Pennsylvania on the twelfth day of March 1755 and is now weak and infirm.
2. He has no record of his age which was recorded in his father's family Bible which some years ago was in the possession of his sister in law. My Humphrey but he does not now know where to find the same.
3. Lived on Gosecreek London County Virginia when called into the service the first time above mentioned and in Geesburg London County Virginia when called into the service the second time above mentioned and continued to reside in Geesburg till the fall of 1782 when I removed to Philadelphia county Pennsylvania and remained there till 1791 when I removed to Bucks county Pennsylvania and lived there till 1791 when I removed to the County of Philadelphia when I continued to reside to the present time and now live. I was called into the service as a drafted militiaman and served as such both times above mentioned except the latter part of the second time when my time having expired during the siege of Yorktown I was requested to remain in the service till the event of the siege and voluntary remained till the surrender of Lord Cornwallis &amp; the delivery of the prisoner to other authority at the Potomac as above mentioned by Col Humphrey.
4. The names of the Regular officers who were with the troops where I served as near as I can recollect and the general circumstances of my service are stated in my declaration above made and the reason an earlier application was not made is I had been told and thought I must go to Virginia to make the application when I served until otherwise informed about two months ago.
