1. Declarant was born in Warminster township Bucks county Pennsylvania on the twelfth day of March 1755 and is now weak and infirmed.

2. He has no record of his age which was recorded in his father's family Bible which some years ago was in the possession of his sister in law, Mrs. Humphreys but he does not now know where to find the same.

3. Lived in Goose Creek Loudon County Virginia when called into the service the first time above mentioned and in Leesburg Loudon County Virginia when called into the service the second time above mentioned and continued to reside in Leesburg till the fall of 1782 when I moved to Philadelphia County Pennsylvania and remained there till 1786. when he moved to Bucks County Pennsylvania and lived there till 1791. When I removed to the County of Philadelphia where I  continued to reside to the present time and now live. I was called into the service as a drafted militiaman and served as such both times above mentioned except the latter part of the second tour where my time having expired during the siege of Yorktown I was requested to remain in the service till the event of the siege and voluntary remained till the surrender of Lord Cornwallas & the delivery of the prisoners to other authority at the Potomac as above mentioned by Col. Humphreys.

5. The names of the regular officers who were with the troops where I served as near as I can recollect and the general circumstances of my service are stated on my declaration above made and the reason an earlier application was not made as I had been told and thought I must go to Virginia to make the application where I served until otherwise service informed about two months ago.
